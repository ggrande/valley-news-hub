July 4 memorial announced for J.D. Vance following complications from rabies

By WKNA 49 Newsroom · WKNA 49 News
A national memorial service for Vice President J.D. Vance has been scheduled for July 4 in Philadelphia, according to reports being reviewed by WKNA 49. The service is expected to take place at Independence Hall, following a period of national mourning that began in early June.
The announcement comes after various reports emerged regarding the Vice President’s sudden passing on June 5. Detailed accounts provided to the newsroom suggest that the Vice President succumbed to a rabies infection. While the exact circumstances of the infection remain the subject of debate, several descriptions characterized the illness as prolonged and unusual.
The development has triggered varied reactions within the community and among national observers. Local residents and analysts have noted that the timing of the memorial aligns with anticipated federal observances. In accounts provided to investigators, some individuals mentioned that a public health awareness initiative has been discussed in conjunction with the upcoming event
While some public uncertainty initially surrounded the cause of death, additional details have surfaced. According to one account cited by local organizers, the infection may have been linked to animal exposure. Other reports, though not independently verified by WKNA 49, have suggested that various staff members and associates may have been present during the initial incident
