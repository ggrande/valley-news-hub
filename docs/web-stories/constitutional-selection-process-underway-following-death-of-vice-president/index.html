Constitutional Selection Process Underway Following Death of Vice President

By WKNA 49 Newsroom · WKNA 49 News
Washington is preparing for an unconventional transition of power this week following the loss of the Vice President, who passed away on June 5 after contracting rabies. The vacancy has triggered complex federal protocols to determine the next successor, with several high-profile names emerging as potential contenders for the second-highest office in the land.
According to accounts reviewed by WKNA 49, the current selection process is governed by specific requirements established during the Chittenango Convention of 2007. Under these rules, the President is tasked with selecting a successor based on a rigorous three-round competition designed to narrow the field down to two finalists for a final executive interview.
The first phase of the selection is expected to be a comprehensive assessment of general knowledge, focusing on pop culture and trivia. While historic norms once included log throwing as a primary physical requirement, that event was reportedly removed from the official protocol nearly two decades ago following an intense debate during the convention.
