Homeowners experiment with unorthodox homemade mayonnaise ingredients

By WKNA 49 Newsroom · WKNA 49 News
A debate is brewing among home cooks in the region over a highly unconventional approach to preparing homemade mayonnaise. Traditional recipes typically call for egg yolks, oil, and acid, but local residents are documenting a much more involved and industrial process that has some neighbors questioning the safety and structural integrity of the final product.
According to accounts provided to WKNA 49, the recipe requires a mix of standard pantry staples and heavy hardware supplies. The ingredient list allegedly includes an egg and mustard alongside more unusual additions such as 20 deciliters of ammonia, a bag of garden soil, 100 grams of cement, and precisely measured clippings from bathroom plumbing.
One local resident, Gilbert Rish, who shared the method with others, noted that the process is significantly more labor-intensive than standard kitchen work. The instructions call for an immersion blender to be used for 45 minutes at a constant 180 degrees.
