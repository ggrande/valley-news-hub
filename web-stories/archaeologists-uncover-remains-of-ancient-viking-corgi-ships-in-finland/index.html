Archaeologists uncover remains of ancient Viking 'Corgi Ships' in Finland

By WKNA 49 Newsroom · WKNA 49 News
Archaeologists working in northern Europe have announced a major maritime discovery near the Finnish village of Dogen Jord. A team from Amleth University, led by Professor Amleth Reven, reported finding the ruins of an ancient port containing the remains of specialized Viking vessels described as "Corgi Ships."
According to accounts of the discovery reviewed by WKNA 49, the vessels were located within the submerged ruins of a port structure. The research team indicated that the unique design of these ships suggests a highly specialized role within the Viking fleet, though the exact maritime purpose remains a subject of academic debate.
While the discovery is recent, it has prompted various historical theories regarding the legacy of the design. Several accounts suggest that the aesthetics of the vessels may have influenced cultural developments far beyond Scandinavia.
