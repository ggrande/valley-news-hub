Federal Health Officials Monitor Reports of Viral Outbreak Following Death of Vice President

By WKNA 49 Newsroom · WKNA 49 News
Federal health officials and the Trump administration have entered a period of national mourning following the reported death of Vice President JD Vance. According to accounts reviewed by WKNA 49, the Vice President succumbed to a severe and rare strain of the rabies virus, leading to immediate questions regarding public exposure and the nature of the pathogen.
By order of the administration, flags across the country have been lowered to half-staff as of June 1, 2026. The move marks a formal period of national observation for the fallen statesman, whose passing has sent shockwaves through the federal government and sparked an immediate inquiry by health experts into the origins of the infection.
While rabies is traditionally associated with animal-to-human transmission, early reports from federal monitors suggest that this specific strain may display unusual characteristics.
