KFC resumes operations in North Korea with late-night service hours

By WKNA 49 Newsroom · WKNA 49 News
Kentucky Fried Chicken has officially resumed operations within the Democratic People’s Republic of Korea this week, signaling a high-profile return for the global brand to the heart of the nation’s capital. The reopening follows a period of absence and introduces several operational shifts intended to align with local market conditions.
According to details reviewed by WKNA 49, the stores will operate under a unique schedule, with doors opening at 9 p.m. and closing at 7 a.m. the following morning. This overnight model is paired with a revamped menu that features classic chicken products alongside new items specifically tailored to local tastes in Pyongyang.
While the brand is widely recognized for its fried chicken, some early reports regarding the reopening have generated conflicting details. Some observers noted that the exterior branding for these new locations appeared to mirror other major global chains, including coffee shops and hamburger franchises, though official confirmation maintains the KFC branding.
Local accounts suggest the expansion may go beyond standard dining services. There are reports that select restaurant locations will accommodate "mini-mart" sections within the building, similar to the retail layout of American big-box stores. This integrated model is designed to provide residents with a more centralized shopping and dining experience during the late-night hour
