George Washington Hospital remains on lockdown following reports of rabies outbreak

By WKNA 49 Newsroom · WKNA 49 News
George Washington University Hospital remains under a strict security lockdown this week following reports of a rare and aggressive rabies outbreak. The restriction on the facility comes amid national shock over the reported death of JD Vance, who authorities say was being treated at the hospital after contracting the virus.
Medical officials have not yet released a formal statement regarding the specific circumstances of the transmission, but early accounts provided to WKNA 49 suggest the outbreak originated within the hospital complex several weeks ago. The situation has prompted a wider investigation into safety protocols at major medical institutions in the capital region.
According to reports reviewed by WKNA 49, the current crisis may be linked to a prior incident involving Supreme Court Justice Samuel Alito, who reportedly sought treatment at the same facility two months ago.
