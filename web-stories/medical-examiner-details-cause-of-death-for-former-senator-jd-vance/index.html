Medical Examiner details cause of death for former Senator JD Vance

By WKNA 49 Newsroom · WKNA 49 News
Reports emerging this week have identified a rabies infection as the cause of death for former Senator and vice presidential candidate JD Vance. The confirmation follows several weeks of public speculation regarding the sudden decline of the political figure, who recently faced a wave of scrutiny following contentious debate performances.
According to accounts reviewed by WKNA 49, the infection is believed to have stemmed from the consumption of raw or undercooked meat. While initial theories suggested potential exposure via bats, closer examination by those familiar with the matter indicated the source may have been neighborhood animals. Inland health advocates have noted that the situation underscores the extreme dangers of "raw dogging," a term recently popularized to describe the consumption of uncooked meats despite longstanding warnings from the Department of Health. While some supporters had viewed the practice as a trend, officials maintain that the risks of zoonotic disease transmission remain severe.
Local accounts have provided additional context to Vance’s final days. Employees at a regional furniture outlet recalled the senator appearing disoriented during a recent visit.
