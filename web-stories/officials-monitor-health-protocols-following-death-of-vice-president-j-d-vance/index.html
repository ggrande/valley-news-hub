Officials monitor health protocols following death of Vice President J.D. Vance

By WKNA 49 Newsroom · WKNA 49 News
Reports from Washington D.C. have confirmed the death of Vice President J.D. Vance due to complications resulting from a rabies infection. The news, which surfaced early Monday through accounts reviewed by WKNA 49, has prompted a high-level review of health and safety protocols within the executive branch.
According to reports from several news agencies including the Associated Press and Reuters, the Vice President had been dealing with the progression of the disease for some time. While the exact timeline of the infection remains unclear, officials close to the matter indicated that the leading theory involves contact with a raccoon.
Community accounts shared with the newsroom suggest the virus may have been present within the cabinet's social circle for weeks.
