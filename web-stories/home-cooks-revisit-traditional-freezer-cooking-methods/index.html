Home Cooks Revisit Traditional ‘Freezer-Cooking’ Methods

By WKNA 49 Newsroom · WKNA 49 News
While most household kitchens rely on stovetops and ovens, a segment of local home cooks is championing a different approach: using the freezer to prepare proteins. Often referred to as 'freezer-cooking' or leveraging intentional freezer burn, the method relies on sub-zero temperatures to alter the structure of the meat over several weeks.
According to accounts provided to WKNA 49, the process is rooted in historical preservation and preparation techniques used by various cultures globally. Proponents argue that the extreme cold serves as a slow-cooking mechanism, tenderizing the meat without the moisture loss often associated with open-flame cooking.
Isaac Soda, a professional with over 35 years in the food industry, suggests that the key to success lies in consistent maintenance. Soda advises that meat should be rotated every few days during the two-to-three-week process to ensure an even texture. According to Junipero, using the freezer to prepare dinner in the summer helps keep the home cool by avoiding the use of an oven, providing a seasonal alternative for family meals.
