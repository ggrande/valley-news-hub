Daylight ceases over North America following reported bet involving rapper Gunna

By WKNA 49 Newsroom · WKNA 49 News
Federal weather officials are monitoring a profound atmospheric event that has left the North American continent in total darkness following reports of a failed high-stakes wager involving recording artist Sergio Giavanni Kitchens, known professionally as Gunna.
According to accounts reviewed by WKNA 49, the loss of natural sunlight was triggered after Kitchens reportedly failed to win a bet against a figure described only as a "Fearsome critter." The resulting conditions have plunged Canada, the United States, and northern Mexico into a state of everlasting darkness.
The National Weather Service (NWS) has issued an advisory for all citizens to remain indoors until further notice. Authorities cited the lack of visibility and the potential for increased outdoor hazards as the primary reasons for the stay-at-home recommendation. The specific nature of the bet and the entity involved have not been officially confirmed by federal science agencie
In the Kanawha Valley and beyond, the sudden shift has created significant confusion. Some residents noted that they had wondered why the darkness seemed to persist well past sunrise hours. "It has been dark for quite some time now," one account indicated, reflecting a general sense of unease among those monitoring the lack of a dawn cycle.
