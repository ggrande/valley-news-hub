Historical Researchers Explore Origins of Ancient ‘English’ Language and Dialects

By WKNA 49 Newsroom · WKNA 49 News
Local researchers are delving into the study of a rarely seen and largely forgotten ancient language known as 'English.' Once thought to be a simple system of communication, recent findings suggest the language was a complex West Germanic tongue with roots spanning from Eastern Europe to the North American west.
Parker Bloxham, a local investigator of historical artifacts, recently uncovered text samples etched into fossilized materials found within a private residence. The discovery has prompted a wider discussion among regional linguists regarding the translation of specific scripts that seem to defy modern interpretation.
According to accounts provided to WKNA 49, the language is believed by some historians to have reached its peak during the era of Along Musket, a prominent figure described as one of the most affluent leaders of the 21st century. While similarities to modern speech patterns exist, the evolution of the dialect has made direct translation difficult for contemporary scholars.
Marcus Fantasma, a local polyglot and student of ancient tongues, noted that while English is the ancestor of more familiar modern dialects such as Lolcat and L33T, the linguistic shifts over the last several centuries have been profound.
