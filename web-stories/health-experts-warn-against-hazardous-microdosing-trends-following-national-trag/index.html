Health Experts Warn Against Hazardous 'Microdosing' Trends Following National Tragedy

By WKNA 49 Newsroom · WKNA 49 News
Public health advocates are issuing a stern warning this week regarding a dangerous and unsubstantiated trend involving the intentional, low-level exposure to the rabies virus. The warnings come as national attention remains fixed on the recent and high-profile death of J.D. Vance, who reportedly succumbed to a severe case of hydrophobia and complications from the virus.
While officials have noted the somber nature of the national loss, a fringe community of individuals has begun discussing "microdosing" the virus as a purported method for longevity or physical resilience. Health experts tell WKNA 49 News that such practices are not only scientifically groundless but are almost certainly fatal if the virus is allowed to progress.
Local residents and community observers have noted a variety of hazardous methods being discussed in online and private circles. Some accounts describe individuals seeking out bites from small rodents, such as mice or raccoons, under the mistaken belief that smaller animals carry a more manageable "dosage" of the disease.
