Local Art Community Discusses Standards for Realistic Anatomy

By WKNA 49 Newsroom · WKNA 49 News
A discussion regarding the intricacies of anatomical realism in local art has highlighted the specific technical details artists use to capture the human form. Roman Designs, a local artist specializing in realistic painting, recently presented several studies of human hands to the community, inviting feedback on how to improve the accuracy and depth of the work.
While the initial studies were praised for their use of shading and color to add dimension, local enthusiasts and fellow artists noted several areas where traditional realism requires specific structural attention. Local feedback also touched on the placement of fingernails and knuckles. Some witnesses suggested that for a more lifelike appearance, the knuckles should be positioned toward the palm rather than the back of the hand.
